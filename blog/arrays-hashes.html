Arrays & Hashes


Arrays and hashes are two very important data structures in computer programming. A data structure is a fancy way of saying how a collection of data is arranged. There are many similarities between these two items, however there are also quite a few differences.

You can think of an array like a row of lockers, and in each locker is a piece of data. This data could be a number, a letter, a String (collection of letters), or even another array (Imagine if you opened one of the lockers and inside was another set of lockers).

A hash can be visualized like a dictionary, with keys and values. In a dictionary, the key is the word and the value is the pronunciation, definition, and sometimes a picture.

In the Ruby programming language, both can be created easily and in a variety of ways. We will highlight two below:

#Ruby
my_array = [1, 2, 3]
my_hash = Hash.new


They are similar in that they can hold a seemingly infinite amount of data. An array can be thought of as hash where the key values are sequential integers. You can access data in these two structures in the following ways:

#Ruby
my_array[0] = 2
my_hash["key"] = "value"


This is just a quick overview of Arrays and Hashes, we encourage you to learn more as your programming career progresses!
